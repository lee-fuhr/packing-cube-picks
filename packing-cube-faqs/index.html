Packing Cube questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

Packing cubes are lightweight fabric containers that divide your suitcase into neat compartments, making packing, unpacking, and finding items far easier. They don't magically shrink your clothes, but they do help you fit more by compressing soft items when you roll or stuff them. For most travelers, a set of two to four cubes in assorted sizes is a genuinely useful upgrade over tossing everything loose into a bag.

What exactly are packing cubes?

Packing cubes are essentially zip-up fabric boxes in various sizes that you stuff with clothes and then stack inside your suitcase or backpack. They come in different shapes – rectangular for suitcases, slim for backpacks – and are usually made of nylon or polyester. The idea is simple: instead of one big jumbled compartment, you get several tidy sections. Most cubes have mesh panels on one side so you can see what’s inside without opening, and some have handles or carry loops. They are not rigid; they flex to fill odd spaces. The most respected brands, like Eagle Creek or Peak Design, are known for durable zippers and water-resistant fabrics, but budget-friendly options from AmazonBasics or IKEA also work well for casual trips.

Do packing cubes actually save space?

This is the most common question, and the honest answer is: it depends. Packing cubes themselves take up a tiny amount of room – the fabric and zipper add almost nothing. The real space-saving comes from how you use them. If you roll each item tightly or use the cubes as compression tools (by squishing air out before zipping), you can fit noticeably more into the same bag. However, if you just fold clothes loosely and drop them in, cubes don’t magically create extra cubic inches. The biggest benefit is organization, not volume. Many experienced travelers report that cubes let them pack more efficiently because they can see everything and avoid over-stuffing one section. In reviews, users consistently say cubes help them fit a few extra shirts or pairs of socks into a carry-on.

Regular packing cubes vs. compression cubes: which should you choose?

Regular packing cubes are simple zip containers – you put clothes in, zip shut, stack. Compression cubes have an extra zipper or a compression strap that lets you push air out, making the cube flatter and denser. Compression cubes are excellent for bulky items like sweaters, hoodies, or jeans, where you can reduce volume by a noticeable amount. But they are usually slightly heavier and cost a bit more. For most trips, a mix is smart: one or two compression cubes for heavy fabrics, and regular cubes for lighter shirts, underwear, and accessories. Keep in mind that compression cubes can wrinkle delicate fabrics more because the clothes are pressed tightly. Brands like Gonex and Veken offer well-reviewed compression sets, while Eagle Creek’s Pack-It line is a longtime favorite for regular cubes. Your choice should match your packing style and the types of clothes you bring.

What size and material should you look for?

Most packing cube sets come in small (for socks, underwear, toiletries), medium (for shirts, shorts), and large (for pants, jackets, or bulky layers). Some brands offer extra-long cubes designed to fit the width of a standard carry-on suitcase. A versatile set includes one large, two medium, and one small – that covers everything from a weekend trip to a weeklong vacation. Material matters for durability and weight. Nylon is lighter and more water-resistant than polyester; both are easy to clean. Look for double-stitched seams and zippers that don’t catch fabric. Mesh panels are great for breathability and visibility but less durable for heavy loads. If you plan to use cubes for backpacking, choose the lightest option – some high-end cubes weigh under an ounce each. For checked luggage, weight is less of a concern, so you can prioritize durability over grams.

How to use packing cubes for maximum organization?

The classic method is to assign one cube per category: all t-shirts in one, all pants in another, underwear and socks in the smallest. When you arrive, you can pull out just the cube you need without rummaging through the whole suitcase. Many travelers also use cubes for separating clean clothes from dirty ones – just toss worn items back into an empty cube. A more advanced technique is to pack by outfit: gather everything for one day (shirt, shorts, underwear, socks) into a single small cube. That way you grab a cube and you’re ready. Compression cubes work best when you roll clothes tightly and then use the extra zipper to squeeze out air. Always pack cubes flat and stack them vertically or horizontally depending on your bag shape. The key is consistency – once you find a system that works, cubes make packing and unpacking a five-minute task.

Are packing cubes worth the investment?

For the vast majority of travelers, yes – especially if you value organization and peace of mind. A decent set of packing cubes costs about what you’d spend on a meal out, and they last for years. The biggest complaint from people who don’t like cubes is that they feel restrictive or add an extra step. But the feedback from long-term users is overwhelmingly positive: cubes reduce packing stress, make security checks easier (you can unzip your bag and see everything), and help you stay neat on multi-destination trips. If you’re a one-bag minimalist who travels with fewer than ten items, cubes might be overkill. But for anyone who packs a typical carry-on or checked bag, they’re a practical upgrade. Reviews on sites like Wirecutter and Pack Hacker consistently rank packing cubes among the most useful travel accessories, often higher than shoe bags or toiletry kits.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use packing cubes for dirty laundry?

Absolutely. Many travelers designate one cube as a dedicated dirty laundry bag – just toss worn clothes in as the trip goes on. Some cubes are made with waterproof linings that prevent smells and moisture from leaking, but even a standard mesh cube works fine for a short trip. You can also buy a separate laundry cube, but a regular medium-sized cube does the job.

Do packing cubes fit in all luggage types?

Yes, they are designed to work with most suitcases, duffel bags, backpacks, and even soft-sided carry-ons. Because cubes are flexible, they adapt to odd shapes and fill empty corners. For slim backpacks, look for cubes that are narrower and shorter – some brands make dedicated ‘backpack cubes’ that are longer and thinner. The vast majority of standard cubes will fit in any bag that has a main compartment at least 10 inches deep.

Are packing cubes waterproof?

Not fully, unless specifically labeled as waterproof. Most packing cubes are water-resistant – they’ll handle a light rain or a spill on the outside of your bag, but they won’t keep contents dry if submerged. A few brands (like Sea to Summit or Osprey) offer cubes made from dry-bag material that can be fully waterproof, but they cost more. For most trips, water resistance is enough; if you’re worried, slip electronics or documents into a separate dry bag inside the cube.

How many packing cubes do I need for a trip?

For a weekend getaway, two cubes (one medium, one small) often suffice. For a weeklong trip, a set of three to four cubes (large, medium, and two small) will let you organize by category or outfit. Frequent travelers often own two sets and rotate them depending on trip length. The sweet spot in expert reviews is usually a 4-piece set – that gives enough flexibility without adding noticeable weight or bulk to your luggage.

Can packing cubes help keep clothes wrinkle-free?

They can help, but they are not a miracle cure. The main wrinkle-prevention benefit comes from packing cubes because they reduce shifting during travel – clothes that stay put wrinkle less than clothes that slide around. Rolling items before placing them in cubes also minimizes creases. However, if you’re packing delicate shirts, you should still fold them flat and place a dry cleaning bag between layers. Compression cubes can actually increase wrinkles for thin fabrics because of the pressure applied.

What are the best-known packing cube brands?

The most frequently recommended brands in travel forums and expert roundups include Eagle Creek (their Pack-It line is a classic), Peak Design (modular and very durable), Osprey (great for backpacks), and eBags (affordable and consistently well-reviewed). For budget shoppers, AmazonBasics and IKEA’s Rensare cubes offer good value. Higher-end options like Tom Bihn or Aer are praised for build quality but come at a premium. The best brand for you depends on your luggage style and budget – all of these have strong followings.

In shortPacking cubes are one of those simple travel tools that, once you try them, you rarely want to go back. They turn a chaotic suitcase into a tidy closet, save you precious minutes at your destination, and actually help you pack smarter. The key is to pick a set that matches your travel habits – whether that’s a budget-friendly starter pack or a premium compression set – and stick with a system that works for you. For the price of a pizza, they can fundamentally change how you feel about packing.
